List of Experiments



  1. To become familiar with the astronomical objects visible to naked eye in the night sky using the software Stellarium. You will create a night sky map at different times. In the map you will identify astronomical objects such as planets, stars, nebula, milky way etc. You will then correlate the objects in the map with the directly observable night sky with naked eye.

  2. To become familiar with the Constellations in the night sky using the software Stellarium. You will learn to identify the constellations at different times (10 PM and 12 midnight) in the night.

  3. To identify the retrograde motion of Mars with respect to the Background stars.

  4. To identify some of the prominent spetral lines in the spectrum of our sun.

  5. To get familiar with the spectra of different stars.

  6. To extract coordinates of a star assuming a telescope in equatorial mount. You will also learn the concept of sidereal time.

  7. To measure astronomical distances using Cepheid variables.

  8. To measure the Proper Motion of Barnard's Star.

  9. To Identify a Circumpolar Star.
